BoldFrankensteinMir



 Could not agree more. This is my favorite Gregory Peck turn of all time. Yeah I know, To Kill A Mockingbird is great, but playing heroes is easy. He's better as Ahab. For the same reasons he's an unforgettable heavy in this, against Laurence freaking Olivier of all people. Adaptation of a best-selling novel with very good thriller editing and thoughtful photography backing all-time acting greats up, it's an amazing movie.

Before we get to it though, I'm really curious about people's reactions to The Last Man on Earth. We've had a week to mull it over, and something I keep thinking about is how Dr. Robert Morgan always slept fully clothed, sitting half-up on his couch just sort of drifting in and out as zombies moaned outside his house. That's a great visual cue of his transformation into Legend. What's scary about him to the new vampire people is that he can go out in the daylight, finding hidden vamps and staking them when they're most vulnerable. But it's the nighttime that proves what a monster he is from their perspective, because he's not hurt by going out in the dark like they are in the light. He defends himself just fine at night, though it comes at the cost of constant vigilance. Sleeping with one eye open, every day a sleep-deprived monotony he's just gotten used to. He hasn't changed out of his vest and jacket combo in weeks, a depressed wreck kind of monster as well as the kind who's impervious to sun damage, never needs vaccine, and has old-world machinery and medical training that let him kill with shocking effectiveness. He fulfills all the aspects of the legendary monster, he's a complete Grendel figure by the end. Such a great story.
